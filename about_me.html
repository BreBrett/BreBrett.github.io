Origins

I am a proud Irish women ,born and bred in Co Mayo in the West of Ireland Mayo Abbey is a small village with approximately 3000 people and is most famousily known for;

  • History: In Mayo Abbey , lies one of Irelands most important Monastic sites in Western Europe, founded by St Colman in 668 AD Mayo Abbey is unique in that it was founded for Saxon monks. To this day the area is known as "Mayo of the Saxons"
  • Naming the County: The name Co Mayo comes from the Irish words "Maigh Eo" meaning "Plain Of The Yew Trees" and originates from the village of Mayo nowadays known as Mayo Abbey
  • The Famine In Mayo most of the population were dependent on potatoes. When the crop failed three years out of four, an estimated one million died through starvation and disease
